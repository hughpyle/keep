# Subjects for Regular Reviewing (AN 5.57)

Upajjhāyasutta. Translated from Pali by Bhikkhu Sujato. CC0 (SuttaCentral).

Five subjects that all beings — woman or man, layperson or renunciate — should review often:

1. **"I am liable to grow old, I am not exempt from old age."** Reviewing this, one gives up the vanity of youth, or at least reduces it.

2. **"I am liable to get sick, I am not exempt from sickness."** Reviewing this, one gives up the vanity of health, or at least reduces it.

3. **"I am liable to die, I am not exempt from death."** Reviewing this, one gives up the vanity of life, or at least reduces it.

4. **"I must be parted and separated from all I hold dear and beloved."** Reviewing this, one gives up desire and lust for the beloved, or at least reduces it.

5. **"I am the owner of my deeds and heir to my deeds. Deeds are my womb, my relative, and my refuge. I shall be the heir of whatever deeds I do, whether good or bad."** Reviewing this, one gives up bad conduct, or at least reduces it.

## All Sentient Beings

"It’s not just me who shall be the owner of my deeds and heir to my deeds. For all sentient beings shall be the owners of their deeds and heirs to their deeds, as long as they come and go, pass away and are reborn".

## Why This Teaching Matters

The fifth remembrance — ownership of deeds — is the ethical foundation for the reflective practice. Actions have consequences. You are what you do. The practice of regular reviewing is itself the mechanism by which bad conduct is reduced and skillful conduct developed.
